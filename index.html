Welcome to DigiPedia

Digimon (デジモン), short for "Digital Monster" (デジタルモンスタ), are mysterious lifeforms that were discovered in the Computer Network in 1997. They are central to the Digimon franchise. The word "Digimon" is typically invariable in the plural, though there are some cases where the term is pluralized as "Digimons". Digimon include almost all the creatures that reside within a parallel universe called the "Digital World." There are many different species of Digimon, as well as unconfirmed and new generations of Digimon yet to be revealed. The number of Digimon species continuously increases as the Digital World expands each year.

Characteristic

Digimon hatch from eggs called Digi-Eggs (デジタマ). They age via a process called "Digivolution" which changes their appearance and increases their physical powers. Some Digimon act feral, like wild animals. Most, however, possess intelligence, speech, and personality traits like humans. It was mentioned that, if a partner Digimon were to be separated from their partner, then they would start to become weaker. An example of this is Yolei Inoue's partner, Poromon. When Yolei is away in Kyoto, Poromon begins to feel weaker each day.

Digimon consist of a DigiCore, a Wire-frame model, and their appearance on top of said model as a texture. As such, there is nothing else other than the wire-frame and texture — for example there is nothing under Angemon's mask other than wire-frame. However, if Angemon's mask were to be ever taken off, its DigiCore would replace its now removed mask with a newly created face.
